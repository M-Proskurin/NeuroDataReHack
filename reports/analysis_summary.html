NeuroData ReHack — analysis summary

Manifold / dimensionality-reduction analysis of two DANDI dandisets from the Jadhav lab (Shin & Jadhav 2023), analyzed in parallel. Generated 2026-07-15.

Headlines. (000447) The novel and familiar W-tracks are different mazes; compared at matched track-relative positions, the cognitive maps show a spatially-structured transformation — clear shared geometry plus real reshaping — and CA1↔PFC geometry is strongly shared. (000978) The population manifold progressively converges toward its final-session geometry as the animal learns, monotonically across 8 run sessions.

Datasets & pipeline

  • 000447 — novel-familiar-novel W-track, CA1+PFC, 5 animals.
  • 000978 — single-day W-track learning, CA1+PFC, 8 animals / 9 files (subject ZT2 = two separate days, excluded from the learning trajectory).

Pipeline (data streamed lazily from DANDI): extraction → linear baselines (PCA/GPFA/dPCA) → nonlinear embeddings (CEBRA/UMAP/Isomap) → dataset-specific comparisons. Headline embeddings use the smoothed fine-bin path (below); dPCA baselines shown at 1000 ms.

Preprocessing (validated). Units are low-rate (CA1 ~1.2 Hz), so hard 50 ms bins are >90% empty and undecodable. The working path is 50 ms bins + Gaussian kernel σ=100 ms + speed > 4 cm/s. σ was chosen by cross-validated position decoding (R² rises from ≈0 at raw 50 ms to ~0.20/0.43 for 000447/000978 with smoothing). Both headlines were re-run on this path and in embedding-independent rate space; the qualitative findings hold (see caveats for the novel/familiar nuance).

000447 — novel vs. familiar

Finding. The novel and familiar W-tracks are physically different mazes, so the map comparison must be made at matched track-relative positions. Done that way, the novel and familiar cognitive maps share clear geometry (disparity 0.585 ≪ 0.906 null, 80% significant; rate space agrees at 0.65 vs 0.80) yet are far from identical — a genuine spatially-structured transformation, not a trivial rate change. CA1 and PFC also share strong spatial geometry.

000978 — within-day learning

Finding. The population geometry converges monotonically toward its final-session configuration over the 8 run sessions (disparity-to-final 0.60 early → 0.16 late) — the geometric signature of within-day W-track learning, robust across bin size (1000 vs 50 ms), representation (rate vs UMAP), spatial binning (2-D grid vs track-relative linearization: 0.46 → 0.13), and region.

Method notes & caveats

  • CEBRA is behavior-aligned (trained on position) so it looks artificially similar/converged; the unsupervised UMAP embeddings carry the honest signals shown here. A CEBRA re-run on the 50 ms path is pending.
  • 000447 novel/familiar must be compared in track-relative coordinates (the two mazes differ). Raw-2-D looks near-chance purely from the maze mismatch; the linearized, arm-matched comparison (and rate space) show clear shared structure. Arm matching uses well identity; the base/choice region is dropped.
  • dPCA / GPFA now use laps as trials. dPCA (1000 ms) picks its regularizer by lap-level K-fold cross-validation and tests the condition/session and interaction variance fractions by permuting the trial label across laps, so the reported fractions are defensible rather than point estimates on an over-fit mean. GPFA fits one trial per lap and indexes latents by linearized track position (see the 000447 GPFA panel), making trajectories interpretable and comparable across conditions/sessions. (We drive the dPCA package only for the fixed-regularizer fit; its built-in auto-regularizer/significance assume a within-trial time axis and are unused.)
  • Comparisons use position-binned centroids on a coarse spatial grid; track linearization would sharpen them.
  • Sleep / replay (000978 6c) abandoned: manifold-occupancy metrics show no co-activation signal at 1000 ms or 20 ms — replay needs SWR-triggered sequence decoding, not this geometry framing.
